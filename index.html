Bio

Hannah Moss is an American lyric soprano currently living and singing in Berlin. She began singing when she was 15 and has taken part in operas and masterclasses in The United States, England, France, Italy, Israel, Belgium, and Germany. Before moving to Berlin in 2015 she studied music and vocal performance at Russell Sage College in New York with teacher Kara Cornell and sang with opera companies Mosaic Arts Opera (Suor Genovieffa in Suor Angelica) and Hubbard Hall Opera (Monica in The Medium and Papagena in Die Zauberflöte).

Directly after graduating college she moved to Italy for three years learning Italian and singing in Tuscany with Lucca Opera Festival, Gli Angeli Custodi (Puccini concerts), Istituto Musicale Baralli (Suor Osmina in Suor Angelica), and was the artist in residence at Villa Baldacini where she sang concerts and private events. Hannah was also accepted to sing with the International Master Class Berlin under Lior Shambadal in 2015 and performed as a soloist with The Diplomatic Choir and The Syrian Expat Philharmonic Orchestra at the Gedächtniskirche. In 2016 Hannah took part in the IVAI Masterclass run my John Norris in Tel Aviv where she performed works by Händel, Mozart, and Puccini. This past fall in Berlin she sang in the Specs On International Feminist Art Music Festival in a piece entitled The Ballad of East Meets West and made her debut with Amici Arts in a night of American opera followed by an opera gala in Antwerp featuring music my Mozart, Puccini, and Donizetti. She is currently performing her first Blanche de la Force (Les Dialogues des Carmélites) under the musical direction of Thomas Cadenbach in Berlin.
